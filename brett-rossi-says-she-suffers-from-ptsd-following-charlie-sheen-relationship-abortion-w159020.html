Brett Rossi Says She Suffers From PTSD Following Charlie Sheen Relationship, Abortion

 Posted on August 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  431 words  |  Martina Birk

Charlie Sheen’s ex-fiancee, Brett Rossi, revealed during an interview with Fox News on Friday, Dec. 4, that she suffers from symptoms associated with post traumatic stress disorder following her relationship with Charlie Sheen.

“For a year, I kept this secret … the reason why I’m speaking now is because I had no voice during our whole relationship and and afterwards … I’m terrified of him,” said Rossi, who has filed a lawsuit against Sheen claiming she was verbally and physically abused during their relationship. “Charlie was so abusive to me and used intimidating tactics to make me fear for my life. Anyone who knows Charlie knows exactly what I’m talking about. I’m terrified of him.”

Sheen revealed he was HIV-positive during an interview on the Today show last month.

As previously reported by Us Weekly, the former adult film star is suing the Two and a Half Men alum for assault and battery, emotional distress, false imprisonment, and negligence. Rossi claims in her lawsuit that Sheen failed to inform her prior to sex that he’s HIV-positive, and that he forced her to get an abortion so that they wouldn’t have a “retarded” child.

“Because of him, I’ve lost my dignity, I’ve had to completely find myself again, I’ve had to go through months and months of therapy. I have nightmares,” an emotional Rossi revealed to Fox News.

“If I have to sacrifice myself to lead the way for every woman to have a voice, then it doesn’t matter [what people think of me] … don’t be scared … because then they win. I’m not scared of the truth and he knows the truth,” she added, explaining that she’s not suing Sheen because of his diagnosis, but because of the alleged abuse she suffered.

“I want justice,” Rossi said. “If i wanted money, I would have kept our child. If I wanted money, I would’ve kept quiet … the reason why I’m going against him in court is because what he did to me and how it will affect me for the rest of my life … the big picture is he has a violent and aggressive behavioral condition that I really hope he seeks help for.”
