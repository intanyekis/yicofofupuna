Jason Momoagushes about stepdaughter Zoe Kravitz in a touching Instagram post

 Posted on July 15, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  759 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Jason Momoa took to Instagram on Thursday to gush about his stepdaughter Zoe Kravitz, after attending the New York premiere of her latest movie The Batman.

The Aquaman actor, 42, posted photos from the event posing with his daughter Lola, 14, and son Nakoa-Wolf, 13, whom he shares with Zoe’s, 33, mother and his estranged wife Lisa Bonet, 54.

‘So excited for everyone to see @thebatman @zoeisabellakravitz your [sic] perfect as cat woman i’m so proud of you,’ he shared.

The actor went on to praise Zoe and pumped up her upcoming Saturday Night Live appearance.

‘So excited for @snl next week your [sic] going to kill it. we had a wonderful time in NYC,’ he said.

Zoe – who portrays Catwoman in The Batman – is the only child of Lisa and musician Lenny Kravitz, 57. 

Jason also acknowledged the fans and the press for giving the family space following his split announcement from the former Cosby Show star in January, after 16 years together.

‘Mahalo to the fans and press for being generous and giving us space for the children it’s hard enough separating in the public eye. we appreciate the continued privacy through these times aloha j.’

Jason looked stylish as ever in the photos from the red carpet and behind-the-scenes snaps with other actors, as he wore a burgundy corduroy suit, which he paired with a plunging green plaid vest.

The Dune star wore his now-standard long hair and thick beard, and matched his suit with burgundy velvet loafers.

Meanwhile Lola cut a stylish figure in a black-and-white checker-print dress, which she wore with black Cuban heels. 

Nakao-Wolf was more low key in a Bob Marley t-shirt under an unbuttoned white dress shirt and a black jacket, which he contrasted with maroon boots. 

In the first photo the actor is seen posing with his arms around his son and daughter as they stand in front of a black Batmobile.

At the premiere Jason spoke about Bonet – who was not able to attend the event – with Entertainment Tonight, saying, ‘Lisa couldn’t be here so we’re representing, me and the babies. We’re very excited to just be here … It’s still family, you know?’

Jason and Lisa announced they had called it quits in a joint statement, in which they explained that while they have grown apart romantically amid the COVID-19 pandemic, ‘the love between [them] carries on’, and they wanted to set an example for their children by freeing each other.
